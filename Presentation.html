What the App does

This app is designed to help newcomers to pharmacology gain familiarity with the concept of the "Dose Response Curve." This sigmoidal curve shows the relationship between a dose of a drug (on the x-axis) versus the Effect (on the y-axis).

The Dose is typically shown on a log scale, using a measure of concentration called Molarity (number of Molecules of the drug per Liter).
The Effect is often normalized (0 to 100%), but typically represents a biological outcome (i.e., amount of tension developed by a piece of muscle tissue).

Understanding this relationship allows us to know how much ibuprofen to take when you have a headache, for example.

Parameters and Units

The formula that describes the dose response curve is given by the equation: \[ Effect(dose) = \frac{Emax * Dose^{h}}{Dose^{h} + EC50^{h}} \]
The h parameter, not yet defined, is a "steepness factor"" called the Hill slope and is unitless
The EC50 parameter is the dose of the drug that gives a half-maximal (50%) effect, and shares the units of dose which are Molarity (moles/liter). A typical EC50 is around 1 nanomolar = \(1x10^{-9}\) M (molar). For this reason, the x scale units are log10 of the dose (and represent just the exponent term, i.e. -9)
The Emax is the maximum effect level. For the purposes of our demo, this is fixed at 100% (typical of a normalized effect scale)

Shiny Sliders for parameter inputs

  • This Shiny App uses sliders to let the user manipulate the EC50 and Hill slope variables, and see the impacts on the shape of the dose response curve in real time.
  • This allows the user to gain familiarity with the impact of these terms. E.g., the Hill slope parameter has an impact on the rate of change of Effect as a function of dose (and therefore, how wide or narrow the range over which the Drug exerts its effect)
